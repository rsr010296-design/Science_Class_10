01 · REFLECTION OF LIGHT

Laws of Reflection & Plane Mirrors

🪞

What is Reflection?

When light falls on a highly polished surface (mirror), it bounces back into the same medium. Light travels in straight lines — evidenced by sharp shadows.

📐

Laws of Reflection

(i) The angle of incidence = angle of reflection: ∠i = ∠r

(ii) The incident ray, the normal at point of incidence, and the reflected ray all lie in the same plane.

Apply to ALL reflecting surfaces — plane AND spherical.

🖼️

Plane Mirror Image

Always virtual and erect. Size = size of object. Image is as far behind the mirror as the object is in front. Image is laterally inverted.

Laws of Reflection — Plane Mirror ★ ACCURATE · NCERT
N = Normal above mirror · N' = Normal below mirror · P = Point of incidence · ∠i = angle of incidence (from normal) · ∠r = angle of reflection (from normal) · 1st Law: ∠i = ∠r · 2nd Law: all three rays coplanar

💡 Important: Both laws apply to ALL reflecting surfaces — concave mirrors, convex mirrors, and any polished surface — not just plane mirrors.

02 · SPHERICAL MIRRORS

Concave & Convex Mirrors

🔴 CONCAVE MIRROR

  • Reflecting surface curved inwards
  • Faces the centre of the sphere
  • Also called converging mirror
  • Focal length: negative (−)
  • Can form real OR virtual images

🔵 CONVEX MIRROR

  • Reflecting surface curved outwards
  • Also called diverging mirror
  • Focal length: positive (+)
  • Always — virtual, erect, diminished
  • Wider field of view

📌 Key Terms — Spherical Mirrors (NCERT exact)

POLE (P)
Centre of the reflecting surface. Lies on the surface itself.
CENTRE OF CURVATURE (C)
Centre of the sphere of which the mirror forms a part. NOT a part of the mirror — lies outside its reflecting surface.
RADIUS OF CURVATURE (R)
Radius of the sphere = distance PC. R = 2f. Focus lies midway between P and C.
PRINCIPAL FOCUS (F)
Rays parallel to the principal axis converge here (concave) or appear to diverge from here (convex) after reflection.
FOCAL LENGTH (f)
Distance PF = R/2. Focus lies midway between P and C.
APERTURE (MN)
Diameter of the reflecting surface of the spherical mirror.
Concave Mirror — P, F and C (accurate geometry) ★ ACCURATE · NCERT
P = Pole (on mirror surface) · F = Principal Focus (midway between P and C, PF = f) · C = Centre of Curvature (NOT on mirror, lies outside, PC = R = 2f)

03 · REFRACTION OF LIGHT

Snell's Law & Refractive Index

💧

What is Refraction?

When light travels obliquely from one transparent medium to another, its direction changes. Caused by change in speed of light in different media.

🏊

Everyday Examples

• Bottom of pool appears raised
• Pencil in water looks bent
• Letters raised under glass slab
• Lemon in water appears bigger

🌊

Key Rule

Rarer → Denser: Bends towards normal (slows down).

Denser → Rarer: Bends away from normal (speeds up).

⚡ Laws of Refraction

1
First Law — Coplanar rays

The incident ray, the refracted ray and the normal to the interface at the point of incidence all lie in the same plane.

2
Second Law — Snell's Law

The ratio of sine of angle of incidence to sine of angle of refraction is a constant for a given colour and pair of media:

sin i / sin r = n₂₁ = constant   (0 < i < 90°)
Refraction through Rectangular Glass Slab — Lateral Displacement ★ ACCURATE · NCERT
At AB (air→glass, rarer→denser): bends TOWARDS normal (∠r < ∠i). At DC (glass→air, denser→rarer): bends AWAY from normal by same amount. Equal & opposite bending → emergent ray parallel to incident ray but laterally displaced.

💡 Key insight: Bending at AB and CD is equal and opposite because the faces are parallel. So emergent ray is parallel to incident ray, but laterally displaced.

📐 Refractive Index Formulae

Absolute Refractive Index
n = c / v
c = 3×10⁸ m s⁻¹ (vacuum), v = speed in medium
n₂₁ (medium 2 w.r.t. medium 1)
n₂₁ = v₁ / v₂
= Speed in medium 1 ÷ Speed in medium 2

⚠️ Optical density ≠ mass density. Kerosene (n=1.44) is optically denser than water (n=1.33) despite lower mass density.

04 · SPHERICAL LENSES

Convex & Concave Lenses

🔭 CONVEX LENS (Converging)

  • Thicker at the middle than edges
  • Converges light rays to a focus
  • Focal length: positive (+)
  • Can form real OR virtual images
  • Used in cameras, microscopes, spectacles

🔬 CONCAVE LENS (Diverging)

  • Thicker at the edges than middle
  • Diverges light rays
  • Focal length: negative (−)
  • Always — virtual, erect, diminished
  • Used in specs for near-sightedness
